Our Curriculum

At Dream's School, we follow a comprehensive curriculum that balances academic excellence with holistic development. Our educational approach is designed to nurture critical thinking, creativity, and a love for lifelong learning.

Primary Section (Classes I-V)

  • Focus on building strong foundations in language, mathematics, and environmental studies
  • Activity-based learning approach
  • Introduction to computers and digital literacy
  • Arts, music, and physical education

Middle Section (Classes VI-VIII)

  • Expanded curriculum with science, social studies, and languages
  • Project-based learning initiatives
  • Introduction to advanced mathematics concepts
  • Sports, performing arts, and extracurricular activities

Secondary Section (Classes IX-X)

  • Preparation for board examinations
  • Career guidance and counseling
  • Specialized focus on core subjects
  • Leadership development programs

Senior Secondary (Classes XI-XII)

  • Streams offered: Science, Commerce, and Humanities
  • Specialized subject teachers with expertise in their fields
  • College and career preparation
  • Competitive exam coaching

Teaching Methodology

Our teaching methodology is student-centered and focuses on developing conceptual understanding rather than rote learning. We employ a blend of traditional and modern teaching techniques to ensure that every student receives the attention and guidance they need to excel.

Key aspects of our teaching approach include:

  • Interactive classroom sessions
  • Technology-integrated learning
  • Experiential and hands-on activities
  • Regular assessments and feedback
  • Personalized attention for different learning styles

Assessment System

We follow a continuous and comprehensive evaluation system that assesses students on various parameters beyond just academic performance. Our assessment approach includes:

Formative Assessments

Regular class tests, quizzes, projects, and assignments to monitor progress throughout the term

Summative Assessments

End-of-term examinations that evaluate overall understanding and application of concepts

Co-Scholastic Evaluation

Assessment of participation and achievement in arts, sports, and other extracurricular activities

Behavior and Values

Observational assessment of discipline, values, attitudes, and social skills

Academic Support

Remedial Classes

Extra support for students who need additional guidance in specific subjects

Advanced Learning Programs

Special programs for gifted students to explore subjects beyond the curriculum

Learning Resource Center

Well-equipped library and digital resources to support independent research and learning

Parent-Teacher Collaboration

Regular meetings and communication to ensure coordinated support for student development
